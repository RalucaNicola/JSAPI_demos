Global Carbon Monoxide January 1st, 2020

Carbon monoxide (CO) is released into the atmosphere primarily as a result of incomplete combustion of fossil fuels, biomass and other organic matter. CO is not considered a significant greenhouse gas.

Natural sources like forest fires or volcanoes can release large amounts of CO in the atmosphere.

2 Widespread agricultural fires are a common occurrence in Central Africa. Read more.

Vehicles produce carbon monoxide as a byproduct of burning gasoline or diesel fuel. Industrial CO emissions can come from manufacturing, mining, oil and gas production.

3 China's emissions of carbon monoxide are mostly given by urban and industrial pollution generated in and around industrialized areas like Beijing. Read more.

4 India has a mixed pattern, pollution spikes because of vehicle and industrial emissions, but crop burning and agricultural fires contribute to the smog in New Delhi. Read more.
