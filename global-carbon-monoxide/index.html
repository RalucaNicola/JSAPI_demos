Global Carbon Monoxide January 1st, 2020

Carbon monoxide (CO) is released into the atmosphere primarily as a result of incomplete combustion of fossil fuels, biomass and other organic matter. CO is not considered a significant greenhouse gas, but it contributes to the creation of tropospheric ozone, an air pollutant that generates smog and is unhealthy.

Natural sources like forest fires or volcanoes can release large amounts of CO in the atmosphere.
